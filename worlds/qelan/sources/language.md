# Sahíric — the Tongue of Degrees (Lešān-Šahír)

The liturgical and philosophical language of Qelán: the tongue in which the Working was once done and in which the codices of the Long Quiet are still written. English name **Sahíric**; in-tongue **Lešān-Šahír**, "tongue of the Radiance."

**Design brief it satisfies:** the _grammar itself encodes the gradient_ — the central cosmological idea (radiance cooling down the Ladder of Degrees) is not merely vocabulary but morphology and syntax. Sahíric has, in the end, **one machine — the Ladder — applied twice**: to things (the degree templates) and to events (the aspects). Flavor draws on Coptic, Hebrew, Greek, and Aramaic registers — the Gnostic acoustic — without being any of them.

---

## 1. Phonology

**Consonants:** m n · p b t d k g q · f s š (sh) ḫ (kh) h ḥ (hard h) · r l · w y · ʾ (glottal stop) · ts **Vowels:** a e i o u, with long ā ē ī ō ū.

**Romanization:** in running text, vowel length is written with the acute (_Šahír_ = _šahīr_); the macron appears only in phonological citation. Stress falls on the long vowel, else the penult.

Acoustic signature: liquid _š/ḥ/q_ clusters, open _-a_ and _-ún/-ím/-át_ endings, glottal _ʾ_ at morpheme seams (_Qenʾah_).

### 1b. Strata — dating a word by its bones

The lexicon has visible geology. Lexicographers of the Collegium date a word by its consonants and its shape:

- **The archaic biliterals** — Q–L (speak), Q–N (descend; rung), Q–M (rise), Š–M (star) — are older than the doctrine. They take reduced forms: the aspect affixes attach to them directly (_qalút_), where triliteral roots re-template (_ḥatmut_, §3), and they never walk the full run of degrees. **The oldest words do not climb the Ladder; the tongue remembers a time before its own theology.**
- **The classical triliterals** are the doctrine-bearing stratum — Š–H–R, Ḥ–T–M, M–Ḥ–Q, S–Q–ʾ — with full degree paradigms (§2).
- **Gemination is augmentative** in the old stratum: Š–M "star," doubled, gives **Šemmá** — "the Star of stars," the Sun.
- **Coinages of the Long Quiet** carry the cold consonants _ts_, _ḫ_, and hard _g_, which are nearly absent from scripture and common in the vocabulary of pressure and mechanism (_ḫol_ coal, _gerets_ engine, _tsanúr_ conduit; see lexicon). Industry does not sound like prayer, and everyone hears it.
- **berzá** (iron) is suppletive and, the lexicographers suspect, a loan: iron fell into the tongue as it fell into the world.

## 2. The signature: roots and the gradient template

Like its Semitic cousins, Sahíric is built on **consonantal roots** (usually three consonants) that carry a bundle of meaning. What is _unique_ to Sahíric is that the **vowel pattern poured into the root marks the word's position on the Ladder of Degrees** (see cosmology §3). The same root, re-voweled, names the same essence at every habitable rung of its descent from radiance to dust.

This is the whole grammar in miniature: **to inflect is to descend.**

### The seven positions (for a root C₁–C₂–C₃)

The tradition counts **seven positions**: five habitable rungs hung between a limit above and a floor below.

|Position|Name|Template|Force|
|---|---|---|---|
|1 · Source|_Šahír_|C₁aC₂**ī**C₃|the essence as undivided radiance — **a limit, not a rung**: the tongue can name what nothing stands on|
|2 · Emanation|_šahar_|C₁**a**C₂**a**C₃|the essence as living, directed light|
|3 · Mind|_šehir_|C₁**e**C₂**i**C₃|the essence as consciousness|
|4 · Life|_šohar_|C₁**o**C₂**a**C₃|the essence quickened, alive|
|5 · Form|_šuhor_|C₁**u**C₂**o**C₃|the essence as wrought, shaped matter|
|6 · Inert|_šahrut_|C₁aC₂C₃**ut**|the essence as dust, fully cooled|
|7 · Cinder|_berzá_|prefix **ber-** / suppletive|the essence finished — **the floor**: real (iron lies on it) but off-path|

Notes on the ends:

- **Position 1 is named, never occupied.** The C₁aC₂īC₃ template exists so that the limit can be spoken of; the Collegium teaches that nothing _stands_ there — to arrive is to stop being a thing (Effacement; cosmology §5).
- **Position 7 is occupied, never reached.** No root derives down to it, just as no natural cooling settles down to it (cosmology §7): true iron is star-ash, and the word for it is suppletive. **The template system covers exactly what natural descent covers; iron is off-template because it is off-path.** For events, the _ber-_ prefix does mark the position (§3) — acts can be cindered even where things cannot cool to it.
- Counting to seven walks the whole span, Source to Cinder, once (see numbers, §6).

**Sound as doctrine.** The melody dims and closes as it descends: the sustained bright _ī_ of the Source; the open living _a–a_; the thin inward light of _e–i_ (the mind's vowels); the rounding of _o_ and _u_ as matter thickens; the stopped, closed _-ut_ of dust. And note the length: **among the degree templates, only the Source's carries a long vowel.** Length is sustain; every descent shortens.

### Worked example — root Š–H–R ("light / to shine")

|Position|Word|Meaning|
|---|---|---|
|1 Source|**Šahír**|the Radiance, the Source|
|2 Emanation|**šahar**|an emanation; a living beam; an aeon's act|
|3 Mind|**šehir**|mind, consciousness, the luminous interior|
|4 Life|**šohar**|a living being; quickened matter|
|5 Form|**šuhor**|wrought matter that still holds light|
|6 Inert|**šahrut**|dust, dead stone, the fully cooled|
|7 Cinder|—|_the root stops here; for the floor the tongue must change words:_ **berzá**|

> A speaker who wants to say "this stone was once alive" does not need an adjective: they shift the vowels one rung up, _šahrut → šohar_, and the listener hears the resurrection in the word itself.

**A canonical near-pair.** The noun _šahar_ "an emanation" and the bare verb _šahár_ "to shine" differ only in the final vowel's sustain. The pair is doctrine in miniature: **an emanation is a shining that has begun to settle.** (The epigraph puns on it; see sample A.)

## 3. No tense — the verb climbs the Ladder

Sahíric verbs **do not mark past/present/future.** Time, to this worldview, is just the cooling of events down the Ladder. So verbs mark **radiant aspect** — _the rung on which the event stands_ — and time falls out of that. Six aspects, spanning the Ladder:

|Aspect|Affix|Rung|Force|Time-feel|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|**Wounded**|prefix **seq-**|through the Source (1)|the act channeled through a séqa — a Working, drawing on the Radiance directly|the magical mode; marked, costly|
|**Lucent**|prefix **ša-**|Emanation (2)|the act _as creation_ — bringing-forth, generative, alive|unfolding / vivid present|
|**Bare**|—|the living rungs (3–4)|the act at the speaker's own height|plain present; gnomic; citation form|
|**Waning**|suffix **-en**|Form (5)|the act _settling_ — completing, cooling into fact|recent / finishing|
|**Sealed**|suffix **-ut**|Inert (6)|the act _as inert truth_ — fixed, done, ossified|distant past / timeless fact|
|**Cindered**|**ber-** + Sealed form|the floor (7)|the act _finished_ — spoken beyond unsaying|the irrevocable|

### One machine, applied twice

The aspect system is not parallel to the degree templates; **it is the degree templates, applied to events.** Every affix is a grammaticalization of one of the tongue's load-bearing nouns:

- **ša-** is the root Š–H–R itself, worn down to a syllable: the act as light.
- **-en** is **qen**, "rung": the act stepping down. (This is why the verb of descent is cited as _qanén_ — Q–N wearing its own cognate suffix. Descent can only ever be spoken as settling.)
- **-ut** is not _like_ the Inert template; it **is** the Inert template. For a triliteral root, the Sealed verb and the degree-6 noun are one and the same form: _ḥatmut_ is both "it is sealed, as fixed fact" and "the sealing, as dust." To seal an event is to nominalize it into stone. (Biliteral roots, too short to re-template, simply take the suffix: _qalút_.)
- **seq-** is **séqa**, the wound.
- **ber-** is **berzá**, the Cinder.

The verb system is built out of **light, rung, dust, and wound** — the four things the cosmology is made of. The bare form is unmarked for the same reason the middle rungs need no flag: **we speak from where we stand.**

**Sound-shape.** The bright aspects leave the stem ringing on its open final vowel (_šaqalá_, _seqqalá_); the dark aspects stop it with a consonant (_qalén_, _qalút_, _berqalút_). To cool a verb is to close its mouth.

### No person, no voice

The Sahíric verb records only _how brightly_, never _who_. There is no marking for person, number, or gender; agent and patient are recovered from word order (§4) and context. The bare form doubles as citation form and as the **gnomic** — the mode of proverbs and law (sample A).

Nor is there voice: verbs are **transitivity-neutral**, active and passive in one form. _Seqá_ is both "to wound" and "to be wounded"; _maḥáq_ both "to efface" and "to be effaced." **The tongue cannot tell cutter from cut.** Given the initiatory lineage of the wound — every opener was opened — this is not a gap but a confession: the grammar has always known what the histories decline to say.

### The archive cannot hold agents

A consequence worth dwelling on: the Sealed aspect is a **resultative** — the event as accomplished fact — and since there is no passive and no agent slot, a Sealed clause _has nowhere to put a doer_. The Annals of the Conflagration are therefore agentless: _te-qiryá maḥqut_, "the city [is] effaced-fact" — and _by whom_ is not omitted but **unsayable in that register**. Grammar as amnesty; the survivors' history rhymes with the _qorbán_ amnesia of the wounded themselves. The codex literally cannot assign blame. (See sample E.)

### The Wounded aspect

**The Wounded aspect is the magic.** Any verb, prefixed **seq-**, becomes a _Working_: the action performed not by mundane means but by drawing raw radiance through the speaker's wound. It carries an automatic connotation of cost (the _qorbán_, the toll of self). To say _seqqalá_ ("speaks-through-the-wound") is to name an act of power and an act of self-harm in one word. Ordinary speakers of the Long Quiet use the Wounded aspect only in quotation, scripture, and curses — to use it sincerely would be to claim a wound. In syntax it **fronts its clause** (§4): you hear the Working before you hear the worker.

### The Cindered aspect

The Cindered form is built _on_ the Sealed form — **ber-** prefixed to the already-ossified verb: _berqalút_, "it is spoken beyond unsaying." Dust first, then the floor: an act can only be cindered once it has sealed, just as (the natural philosophers note, approvingly) nothing reaches iron except through death. And as true iron comes only of a star's dying, a Cindered utterance is had only at ruinous authority: it appears in the Wardens' sentences of sealing, in oaths sworn on one's own séqa, in curses meant to outlast the curser. Ordinary speech never touches it.

Wounded and Cindered are thus the two **marked poles** of the verb — the aperture and the floor — and the script mutilates the letter for each (§7).

### Worked example — root Q–L ("to speak / to work by voice")

|Aspect|Word|Meaning|
|---|---|---|
|Bare|**qalá**|speaks (person from context); _to speak_ (gnomic/citation)|
|Lucent|**šaqalá**|speaks-creating (the words bring something forth)|
|Waning|**qalén**|has just spoken (the speech settles into fact)|
|Sealed|**qalút**|it is spoken / so it was said (fixed, scriptural)|
|Wounded|**seqqalá**|speaks _through the wound_ — a Working by voice (the _seq-_ geminates before _q_)|
|Cindered|**berqalút**|it is spoken beyond unsaying (a sentence, an oath)|

## 4. Syntax: word order _is_ the Ladder

The ordering rule is the cosmology again: **constituents are spoken in descending order of degree.** The most radiant thing in the sentence comes first; the most cooled comes last. There is no fixed Subject-Verb-Object; there is _high-to-low_.

- A sentence about the sun acting on stone names **Šemmá** first, then the act, then the **šahrut**.
- A sentence in which a stone acts upon a mind — a metaphysical scandal, since the cooled should not command the radiant — is _grammatically marked_: it requires the **inversion particle `men-`** ("against the Ladder") fronting the low-degree agent, the way some languages mark an unexpected or blasphemous claim. A poet uses _men-_ to say "the dust commanded the king" and the wrongness is audible. Under the law that native light only falls (cosmology §5, §7), _men-_ marks exactly those Workings that leave their object _higher_ than it began — hence **men-qol**, the against-Working, the breaking of iron.

### Where the verb stands

The verb is a constituent like any other: **it sorts at its aspect's rung.**

- **Wounded** (rung 1) and **Lucent** (rung 2) verbs therefore _open_ their clauses — you hear the light before you hear who bore it. (Witnesses of Workings report the same of the deed itself.)
- The **bare** verb walks beside its speaker, directly after the agent: we act from where we stand.
- **Waning**, **Sealed**, and **Cindered** verbs sink toward the clause's end.
- Where verb and noun share a rung, **the verb precedes**: light moves before it settles.

One conventional exception: the Ladder itself, and frames like it, sort **by the foot** when named in a clause. The traditional teaching: _"the Ladder hangs from the Radiance but stands on the dust; the Source needs no ladder."_ (So in the codex's first line, _pe-Qenʾah_ follows the verb; sample B.)

Other syntax:

- **Articles run on the Ladder, not on gender:** **pe-** (sg., for what burns or grows — positions 1–4), **te-** (sg., for what is wrought or still — positions 5–7), **ne-** (pl., all degrees). _pe-Šahír_ "the Radiance," _te-qiryá_ "the city," _ne-ḥatumím_ "the Sealed Ones."
- **The low pronoun _is_ the low article.** _Te_ "you (to a cooled thing)" and _te-_ "the (of cooled things)" are one word: to address a person as _te_ is to point at them with the grammar of objects. The insult is structural. There is no politeness system; there is only the Ladder.
- **Genitive = construct (juxtaposition), head first — [thing]-[origin]:** _lešān-šahír_ "tongue-of-Radiance," _Sefer-Qenát_ "Book-of-Degrees," _šahrut-Hú_ "dust-Its," _Petáḥ-Séqa_ "Opener-of-the-Wound." Because a thing's origin stands higher on the Ladder than the thing itself, a construct read aloud is a small **ascent**: the clause descends, but its phrases climb, each noun-chain recapitulating emanation in reverse. The scribes call the construct _"the little ladder."_
- **Copula:** usually **zero** — equation is shown by juxtaposition and a spoken pause (written with a dash). _Qelán šahrut-Hú_ "the world [is] Its dust."
- **Possessive suffixes:** _-i_ (my), _-hi_ (her), _-hú_ (his/its), _-an_ (our). By convention, written **-Hú** when the antecedent is the Radiance.
- **Conjunction / negation:** **wa-** "and" (proclitic); **ló** "not."
- **Pronouns encode degree/animacy:** _an_ I, _anún_ we, _tā_ you (to an equal or higher — a mind, the sun, a seqál), _te_ you (to a cooled thing; see above), _hu/hi_ he/she (a mind-bearer), _za_ it (cooled), **Hú** the divine It (the Radiance).
    - Choosing _te_ for a person is an insult (you file them among things); choosing _tā_ for a stone is either prayer or madness.

## 5. Sample translations (interlinear)

**A — Epigraph of the wound** (the line every seqál knows)

```
Seqá   —   šahár ;   šahár   —   naší.
wound.BARE — shine.BARE ; shine.BARE — forget.BARE
```

_"To be wounded is to shine; to shine is to forget."_ (Zero copula; the dash is the equational pause. Three bare gnomics chained — the most compressed sentence-type, reserved for proverbs and law. Note the voice-neutrality: _seqá_ is "to wound" and "to be wounded" at once; the law does not care which end of the knife you hold. And note the pun the tradition treasures: heard with _settled_ vowels, the opening equation is _séqa — šahar_, "the wound [is] an emanation." Recited quickly, the law of the wound and the physics of the wound are one sentence.)

**B — The first line of the codex** (cosmological)

```
Pe-Šahír     qanén        pe-Qenʾah,   wa Qelán  šahrut-Hú.
the-Radiance descend.WANE the-Ladder,  and world dust-Its
```

_"The Radiance descends the Ladder, and the world is Its dust."_ (High-to-low: _Šahír_ (position 1) opens. _qanén_ = Q–N "descend" + Waning _-en_ — the descent settling into the standing fact of the world — and the Waning verb stands at its rung, mid-clause. _Pe-Qenʾah_ follows the verb because frames sort by the foot (§4). Second clause: zero copula; _-Hú_ capitalized, for the antecedent is the Radiance — the world is **Its** dust.)

**C — A Warden's seal-formula** (ritual of the Ḥatumín)

```
Be-berzá   pe-qol     ḥatmut ;     pe-ḥatúm       ló  šahár.
by-iron    the-voice  seal.SEALED ; the-sealed.one not shine.BARE
```

_"By iron the voice is bound; the Sealed does not shine."_ (_ḥatmut_ = Ḥ–T–M in the Sealed aspect — which for a triliteral root simply _is_ the degree-6 template: the binding as accomplished dust, exactly the tone a ward wants. _Pe-qol_ takes the living article: a voice is a mind's act. _Pe-ḥatúm_: the patient noun, a mind still — hence _pe-_, even in chains. But mark the first two words: the formula is **deliberately inverted**. Iron, lowest of things, fronts the sentence, and the _men-_ that inversion demands is conspicuously refused — for Warden doctrine holds that sealing is not _against_ the Ladder but its **completion**, a helping of light toward rest; their tract on the point is called _pe-Ḥatím_, "the Seal in the Source," the Source-template of Ḥ–T–M — scandalous vowels for an orthodox claim. Collegium grammarians call the liturgy ungrammatical. The Order answers that its grammar is its creed.)

**D — A Working** (showing the marked aspects fronting, and the article changing)

```
Seqqalá       pe-seqál ;        šaqúm       pe-šuhor.
WOUND.speak   the-wounded.one ; LUCENT.rise the-wrought.matter
```

_"The wounded one speaks through the wound; the stone wakes into light."_ (Both verbs open their clauses, as their rungs demand — the Wounded act through the Source, the Lucent act as Emanation: in a Working you hear the deed before the doer. And note _pe-šuhor_: strict grammar wants _te-_ for wrought matter. The _pe-_ is **proleptic** — the Working grants the stone the living article as it rises. In the old texts one hears the grammar change before the stone does. Cosmologically the rising is a _release_, paid from the stone's own store or the speaker's self — no _men-_ is needed, for the stone will end no higher than it began; native light only falls.)

**E — From the Annals of the Conflagration** (chronicle style)

```
Te-qiryá   maḥqut ;         ló  šohar,           ló  šuhor —          šahrut.
the-city   efface.SEALED ;  not living.thing,    not wrought.thing —  dust
```

_"The city is effaced; nothing lives, nothing keeps its shape — dust."_ (The Sealed verb admits no agent — not omitted: **unsayable**. The Annals record a war with no combatants; grammar as amnesty. The enumeration then walks the rungs downward, as syntax requires, and stops at _šahrut_ — spent dust, the glass the great battles left; even effacement cannot make iron (cosmology §7). _Te-qiryá_: the city, a wrought thing, takes the cooled article — the chronicler will not even flatter what he cannot mourn.)

## 6. Core lexicon (~70 entries)

### Cosmology & the Ladder

- **Šahír** — the Radiance; the Source
- **šahr** — radiance (the substance, light)
- **Šahrún** — the Fullness; the realm/condition of pure radiance (_šahr_ + old collective/locative _-ún_)
- **Qenʾah** — the Ladder of Degrees
- **qen** (pl. _qenát_) — a degree, a rung; also "seven" (see numbers)
- **šahar** — emanation; living beam (position 2)
- **šehir** — mind, consciousness (position 3)
- **šohar** — a living being; quickened matter (position 4)
- **šuhor** — wrought matter (position 5)
- **šahrut** — dust, dead stone (position 6)
- **berzá** — iron; the Cinder; the floor of the Ladder (position 7). Suppletive — no root cools down to it — and, the lexicographers suspect, a loan (§1b)
- **qanén / qum** — to descend / to rise (Q–N / Q–M; on the form _qanén_, see §3)
- **Qelán** — the world (_qen_ + old locative _-án_: "the place of rungs" — the same suffix family that builds _Šahrún_; the Fullness and the World are named as the two places)

### Stars & emanations

- **Šemmá** — the Sun (the near aperture; a single vast mind; augmentative gemination of Š–M — "the Star of stars," §1b)
- **šem** (pl. _šemím_) — a star; a distant aperture of the Radiance
- **qadína** (pl. **Qadín**) — an emanation of the solar mind; a Visitant (the plural keeps the archaic _-ín_: the oldest beings wear the oldest grammar)
- **nūr** — a lamp; a flame

### The wound & the Working

- **séqa** (pl. _seqím_) — the wound; the puncture onto the Šahrún
- **seqá** — to wound-open; to be wounded-open (S–Q–ʾ; transitivity-neutral, §3)
- **seqál** (pl. _seqálim_) — a wounded one; a puncture-bearer ("the Wounded," "the Lucent")
- **qol** (pl. _qolím_) — a voice; an utterance; hence a Working done by voice. In the old tongue every _qol_ is potentially a Working — there is no word for powerless speech; the Long Quiet uses it for plain "voice," and the philologists note that the word itself has cooled
- **ʾot** — a Working done by gesture; a Sign
- **šur** — a Working done by beholding; the gaze-channel, rarest of the three (whence the Sealed are masked across the eyes; cosmology §6)
- **men-qol** — an _against-Working_: forcing matter back up the Ladder against its own settledness; the canonical instance is the breaking of iron (see §4, _men-_)
- **qalá** — to speak; to work by voice (Q–L)
- **qorbán** — the toll; the forfeit of self burned by a Working; an offering
- **naší** — to forget; to lose self (N–Š–Y)

### The Effaced & the Sealed

- **maḥáq** — to efface, to consume; to be effaced (M–Ḥ–Q; transitivity-neutral)
- **maḥúq** (pl. _maḥuqím_) — an Effaced one; a mind consumed into the Source (patient template C₁aC₂ūC₃)
- **ḥatám** — to seal, to bind (Ḥ–T–M)
- **ḥatúm** (pl. _ḥatumím_) — a Sealed One; an imprisoned seqál (patient template)
- **Ḥatumín** — the Wardens of the Seal; the Order. The _-ín_ is the archaic liturgical plural, frozen in titles — and note what the title says: the Wardens name themselves with the **patient-plural of "seal."** Each Warden is ritually self-bound; the Order keeps the cage by entering it
- **pe-Ḥatím** — "the Seal in the Source": the Wardens' doctrine that sealing completes the Ladder rather than opposing it (the Source-template of Ḥ–T–M; see sample C)
- **Berzhár** — an iron-mount; a great prison-mountain (_berzá_ + _har_)

### People, places, things

- **lešān** — tongue, language
- **sefer** — a book, a codex
- **har** — a mountain
- **qiryá** (pl. _qiryát_) — a city, a settlement
- **Veshpor** — a city of the Long Quiet (seat of the Collegium)
- **Petáḥ-Séqa** — "Opener-of-the-Wound" (title of the First Opener)
- **Ḥatám-Qol** — "the Sealing of the Voice": byname of Toren, founder of the Ḥatumín ("Sealwright" is the Collegium's rendering; the _-wright_ names are translation convention for construct-bynames)

### Coinages of the Long Quiet (the cold stratum, §1b)

- **ḫol** — coal
- **gerets** — an engine; a pressure-work
- **tsanúr** — a pipe, a conduit
- _No scripture contains ts. Scholars date a word by its consonants._

### Grammar words

- **pe- / te- / ne-** — the (sg. living / sg. cooled / pl.); see §4
- **wa-** — and · **ló** — not · **be-** — by, with (instrumental)
- **men-** — _inversion particle_: marks a low-degree thing acting against the Ladder; combined with a verb it names an against-Working (e.g. _men-qol_, the breaking of iron)
- **an / anún** — I / we · **tā / te** — you (high / low; _te_ is the cooled article turned on a person, §4) · **hu / hi** — he / she · **za** — it (cooled) · **Hú** — the divine It
- **-i / -hi / -hú / -an** — my / her / his·its / our (_-Hú_ when the antecedent is the Radiance)

### Numbers (base-seven, after the seven positions)

- **waḥ** 1 · **tan** 2 · **šel** 3 · **rab** 4 · **ḥem** 5 · **set** 6 · **qen** 7 (also "a full Ladder")
- Seven is _qen_ — the same word as "degree/rung": **to count to seven is to walk the Ladder once, Source to Cinder.** Beyond seven, counting "begins a new Ladder": **qen-wa-waḥ** = 8 ("a Ladder and one").
- **There is no zero.** A cosmology with no empty counts nothing as "none"; all sums are additive. When the Collegium's mathematicians at last borrowed a cipher for their pressure-tables, they wrote it as **the iron bar** — a strikethrough with no ray beneath it (§7) — and the pious still refuse to write it.

## 7. The script (described, for the artifact's glyph plate)

Sahíric is written in **rayed glyphs**: each consonant is a vertical stroke (a "ray"), and the **vowel-melody — i.e. the position on the Ladder — is marked by dots placed along the ray's height.** High dots = high degree (radiant); low dots = low degree (cooled). The _same consonantal skeleton_ therefore looks visibly "brighter" when it names a higher rung — the writing system, like the grammar, makes the Ladder legible at a glance. Because dot-height is degree, **every page has a skyline**: scripture glitters near the tops of its columns; ledgers and inventories sit low.

The two marked poles of the verb are the two **mutilations of the letter**:

- **Sealed and Cinder forms** are written with the ray _struck through_ — a bar of iron across the light.
- **Wounded forms** are written with the ray _broken_ — a gap in the stroke, an aperture in the letter itself.

The borrowed zero (§6) is the bar with no ray at all: iron across nothing.

Text runs **top-to-bottom**, then left-to-right — reading is itself a descent down each column from radiance toward dust.
