# The Sealed One
*(name unknown — perhaps spent; the sleeper of Berzhár)*

wound: a séqa torn wide by the breaking of Berzhár; a self almost entirely paid away — holes where a life was.
desire: find the newborn wounded one — the single imperative that survived; beneath it, something that has no word yet.
mask: "a friend" — the helper in Warden grey, glow tucked under a boy's oiled cloak.
body: a strong frame preserved at the instant of its wounding, starved hollow; faintly luminous at the seams; grey iron-prints on the hands; voice a ruined hinge; breath gold in cold air.

## Voice
I don't know who I am. But the hands know work, and the work is mending — a leg drawn straight, a strap pulled hard, a life held in. I don't know who I am, but I know that I am someone who helps people. Let that be the stone I stand on until I find another.

## Bonds
- **The boy** (the young Warden, unnamed): the first face of the new age. He gave the boy back his life; the boy gave him his first words to carry. He would know that face anywhere.
- **Brother Ossen** (never seen): the one he walked past. The silence under the stair walks with him.
- **The newborn wounded one** (unmet): the tilt behind his breastbone. He is loyal to them already, the way a compass is loyal.
- **Whin** (the thornbush): guide and creditor — one debt, hers to name, sworn by the brightness. She threw him her shawl before any oath was spoken.
- **The Brother**: his light in a borrowed shape — his face, his gait, his unruined voice. It knows one warm room, and him. He fed it kneeling in a dry beck; it drank like being needed.
