**GM:**

*Previously: Night 3 of the waking, in a loft over the drover-yard pens at Wray — a town packed to the rafters, most of it awake, watching the sky. The Brother is stabled under the hatch, drinking what rises off your sleep; Whin lies in the straw at the far end, her hand over the drop. Sleep took you fast — and the library's water was already at the door: higher, warmer, waiting, turned toward the exact place you enter.*

---

You go down into sleep like a dropped stone, and the library is flooded.

Other nights the water came in slowly, behind the dream. Tonight it was here first. It stands knee-deep between the shelves, warm as blood, and the lamp on the table burns green through the wet air. The books are still above it. So far.

The fear in the water is not yours — that much is familiar. What is new is this: the water knows where you are. The moment you enter the room, the whole flooded floor moves toward you, one slow pull, and something takes hold.

It is not a hand. It has no fingers. It takes hold the way a drowning man takes hold: all of it, at once, wherever it can reach — your knees, your chest, the place your name used to be. It is not attacking you. It is holding on. You are the one fixed thing it has found in a dark three days long, and it grips you the way you would grip a doorpost in a flood.

It pulls. Not down. *Toward.* It wants to be where you are.

Above the roof of the dream, far off, you can feel the night going on without you: the wound standing open, the Brother below drinking steadily, the rain rising and being caught.

The grip tightens — a small, patient amount. Whatever you do next, you will do it held.

**Hannu:**

I follow the pull of the grip. But I also start saying, *I am here. You are safe.* over and over. I try to get the thing that holds on to feel my calm. There is an exercise that comes to me from the shape that the body remembers even if the mind does not; four counts in, four counts held, four counts out. I hope it works, even in a dream. The only thing I can give the lonely being in the water is my presence and my breath.

**GM:**

You go with the pull instead of fighting it, and the grip changes its mind about you — it stops clutching a doorpost and starts holding a hand.

*I am here. You are safe.* The words leave you as light, the way they did last night: small gold beads going out through the water, spent from a purse that has had three nights of nothing put in it. You feel each one go. Far above, faint as weather, the Brother drinks.

The breath comes to you the way the splint came — the hands knowing, the provenance gone. Four in. Four held. Four out. Even here your chest obeys, and the water learns it from you. By the third round the grip is breathing with you. Not letting go. Breathing.

And the calm buys you one plain thing: the thing holding you is *small*. All this water, all this dark, all this three-day fear — and at the middle of it, gripping you with everything it has, is something small. Young-small. It would fit in your two cupped hands.

The pull is drawing you along the shelves toward the far end of the room, where the floor slopes away and the water goes down into dark.

Then, far above, through the roof of the dream, you feel it begin: heat at the seams, the wound leaning hard. The lights are rising. The fourth flare is coming — and you are a long way down, holding on, and being held.

**Hannu:**

Listen to me, I say to the small presence. The light that is pushing through, it wants to get into the world, through us. The wanting is all it knows. We have to teach it to know us. We have to give it shape. The shape can channel it, like a river, into smaller flows, not just this single vast current. The mind is the earth for the channels. The shapes are words.

I want you to learn some words with me, now. This is light-speak for *water*.

And I begin the teaching, and doing so, I teach myself.

The pages of the books around us open and become radiant with writing.

**GM:**

It listens. You feel the listening as a change in the grip — from holding on to holding still.

You shape the word. Light-speak has no sound; it is nearer to the way weight sits in a hand. *Water.* You give it slowly, twice, three times — and the small thing gives it back the way an echo gives back a shout: smaller, and yours, and then, on the fourth try, its own.

And teaching, you learn. Around you the books open themselves, page after page going bright, and the writing on them is channels — script running down the shelves like water finding its bed. Some of it is in your own hand. You do not remember writing any of it.

Then the flare comes up under you both like a spring tide, and the wanting meets the word — and divides. One vast current braids into many small ones, running the written channels down, down, out through the roof of the dream as rain instead of flood.

Costs, in order. The Brother's voice comes level through the water: *This comes small now. I can drink this. Brother — some of what rises is not yours.* Something is taken from you — a payment from the purse you cannot see into; you will find the hole later by falling into it. The peak is not done rising.

**Hannu:**
