The word hangs in the opened grave between you.

You are on your knees at the chamber's lip, bare-chested, dripping, the borrowed noon long spent. Down the round dark the water shows your own guttering gold back at you — and hers, thin at every seam, and her eyes.

The chamber is filling faster since the slab came off. You can hear it: a new note under the old drip, water finding your opening and using it. It stands at her chin. It will not stay there.

Around you the ring's teeth lean black against a sky going grey — the first sky in five nights with nothing in it, and a whole country out there watching it stay empty. The iron drinks at you steadily, a draught through an open door.

Between this island and the shore lie three hundred paces of black winter water. On the far side of them Whin stands at the pack, small and still, and the light-horse stands beside her because you told it to, and neither of them can reach you here.

Her one hand keeps its grip on the highest rung — four days on the thing that has been drinking her, and she has not let go of it yet. She holds on, and looks up at you, and waits for the word to come back true.
