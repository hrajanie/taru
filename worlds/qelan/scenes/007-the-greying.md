Behind you on the shore, two tins knock once against their buckles — *tap*, a knuckle on a door — and go still. One belongs to the gleaner father, busy with a knot. The other to a man asleep by his fire. Nobody turns. Whin has already found both belts with her eyes and priced them.

"Spent some luck," she says, low. "Tell me before, next time."

The smaller boy is tugging his brother's sleeve — *it floated, it floated* — and the brother, nine and worldly, delivers the verdict that will guard you better than any Working: "Stones don't float."

No toll that you can find. The word wanted saying; the stone paid its own way down.

Then it is the leaving, all at once, the way leavings are: packs balanced and lashed, the blaze muddied, the rope halter hanging loose on a neck that could snap it by breathing. The Brother folds itself down onto the turf beside Qamí — a hill deciding to be a stair — and she looks from its back to you, lit all the way through, one hand already full of mane.

Whin swings her own pack up. "Midmorning," she says. "We're walking."
