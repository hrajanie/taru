# Canon — Qelán
*Facts established through play, cited by scene. Scripture lives in `sources.md` and is not repeated here.*

## People
- **The Sealed One** (unnamed; sheet: `characters/sealed-one.md`) — woke beneath and broke Berzhár at dawn; retains one imperative (find the newborn wounded one) and the craft of mending; first words in eight centuries: "A friend." (sc 001)
- **The boy** (unnamed young Warden, ~17, brick-carrier) — pinned under seal-iron in the collapse; freed, femur set and splinted by the Sealed One; kept the work-knife; witnessed everything; will say *"a friend came out of the mountain."* (sc 001)
- **Brother Ossen** — Warden, buried where a brick-slide swallowed the stair two turns below; silent when the Sealed One left; fate unknown. (sc 001)
- **The Warden climbers** — eight to ten, single file, disciplined, chanting the seal-formula as they climbed; two carried something long (litter or ram, unresolved). (sc 001)
- **Whin** (wear-name; born name withheld; sheet: `characters/whin.md`) — scavenger-goatherd of Harrick's steading under Coldshoulder, ~16, wind-burned; rings iron against the unquiet; found and kept a fallen dream-drop; guided the Sealed One to the beck and the shieling; holds one debt, hers to name. (sc 002)
- **The Brother** (sheet: `characters/the-brother.md`) — born in the dry beck of the night's dream-leakage, fed to completion with poured light; wears the Sealed One's face, brow-scar, gait, and unruined voice; first words "I don't know who I am"; sole memory "You made the room warm"; burns what it holds. (sc 002)
- **Carse** — peat-cutter over the shoulder, Millbeck side; forty-odd, heavy, two dead fingers on the left hand; "owes money the way other men have lice." Squatted in Harrick's shieling after the fall; gleaned a quarter-tin of drops and surrendered them to "Warden business"; went down to the column with his tale. (sc 003)
- **Ma Sorrel** — old woman of the steading under the far end of the hawthorn line; trades with Harrick's house; sold bannock, cheese, and dried fish for Whin's salt; source of the vale's news. (sc 003)
- **The gleaner boy** — ~12, gleaner's satchel, Wray road; traded his good knife for three drops; his tin led him to the horse, which ate them; paid a coin by Whin; the tale of the horse that ate the stars starts in his mouth. (sc 004)
- **The drover woman** — kind hands, a pen at Wray; bought Whin's goat at nearly the named price; the loft over her pens is night 3's roof, a penny. (sc 004)
- **The star-glass dealer** — a neat man at Wray market: coin-scale, ledger, iron-lined box; tests drops on his open palm and pays weight; "The Order pays nothing and calls it duty — *I* pay weight"; sells on down the river. (sc 004)
- **The way-house brothers** — two grey brothers at Wray's ford crossing, watching the crowd "for a particular fish"; one holds something small and shut, cupped; the other went inside to a desk — a report written: something passed the ford this evening. (sc 004)
- **The preacher** — full-voice on a barrel in Wray's market square, arms bare to the elbow, unstopped in murmur country: "The seals are old, and the sky remembers!" (sc 004)
- **Mardy** — of the dye-yard by the lower ford ("everyone knows the yard"); her sister two winters dead; three nights asking door to door for the sister's boy; "Tell him Mardy's not cross." (sc 005)
- **Fen** — Mardy's sister's boy, thirteen, reddish, gap in his teeth; went up to the lights the first night; three nights gone; his tin found at the near ridge, open, empty, set down neat on a stone like an offering. (sc 005)
- **The whitesmith girl** (unnamed texture) — soot to the elbow at a market-fringe brazier, lining gleaners' tins with hammered iron and crying it dry as a landlord: "Iron your tin! Quiet your glass or let it sing, penny the lining, your choice!" The one honest trade the lights have made. (sc 005)
- **The young way-house brother** (unnamed — contacted on-screen, never named aloud) — Order grey, sharp-eyed, a little breathless, notebook in hand; searched the drover-yard stall through the qel-qelá and his nose nearly won; wrote as he walked; summoned off by the bronze bell; stood at the yard gate at last with a boy pointing at the pens. (sc 005)
- **The child** — the newborn wounded one, found: girl-shaped, eight or nine, thin as a lath, gold leaking at every seam of her the way it leaks at his; four days under a slab, one hand gripping the highest rung of an iron ladder that drank at her; owns three taught words (*water*, *tanút*, *qam*) and one kept image — a hillside of whin in bloom. Sheet: `characters/the-child.md` (stub, new). (sc 005)
- **Qamí** (wear-name, lent — his Sahíric diminutive of *qam*, "little rising one") — given at the water's edge and taken: she pressed her free hand flat to her own chest "the way you would press a coin to keep it." Held on Whin's terms: "Ours to lend. When she finds her own name — or takes one — she gives it back, no debt." New words: *yedá* (hand), *patá* (open) — and *rafá* (mend), given, unused: too heavy for embers. She would not take food and would not leave his side; her one strong aversion is the shelf's things. (sc 006)
- **The builder** — the man in the book's entered day: a master of works, forty-odd by the shoulders and older by the way the body carries time; working hands with no iron-prints; drinks shallow, counts heads without meaning to (fourteen), mends what cannot be left torn. The hand is his. (sc 006)
- **The keeper of the long house** — face and name eaten out of every line and out of the memory itself; kept the roof and the fire winter after winter; "I stay because you can't stop, and somebody has to feed the man who can't."; "What would you be, the morning it was done?" (sc 006)
- **Qamí, her speech** — she retains most of the common speech of the world; production is slow and patchy, "a coin at a time from a deep pocket," with burned places where wound-adjacent words lived — a burned place is not a forgotten word ("you know the difference from inside"), and she goes around them: "Water. Gone." New common words taken as her own: *morning*, *later*, *together* — and *secret*, recognized ("she knows that word from somewhere"); *safe* half-recognized. (sc 007)
- **Qamí, the first laugh** — she paid the language game back: a pebble on her palm, his own *watch* look given back, "Qam" — the pebble hopped a hand's height, and she laughed, "loud, up and out of her the way the lark went, glad all the way through." Her first. (sc 007)

## Places
- **This Berzhár** — split stem to crown; a great stair descends the cloister-side face; the west flank is trackless rust-cliff and scree, a full day's broken country from any road. (sc 001)
- **The cloister** — walled Warden house at the mountain's foot: grey stone, black roofs, forge-chimneys; one wing crushed by a thrown slab; its bell rang and then stopped. About an hour's hard descent below the upper stair. (sc 001)
- **The plain** — brown, beyond the foothills: a straight road, a river crossed by a stone bridge, a distant town under standing coal-smoke; past it, southwest, haze to the horizon. (sc 001)
- **The Reckoner's tower** — the roofless old watch-post on the west flank (locals' name); dead hearth, stone cistern; its hearthstones now carry two ash-pale handprints. (sc 002)
- **The dry beck** — a snowmelt channel south along the west scree, banked in dead thorn; birthplace of the Brother. (sc 002)
- **The shieling under Coldshoulder** — Harrick's summer hut, two hours southwest of the tower: dry-stone, turf roof, hearth, empty goat-fold; the door found disturbed — someone's been in. (sc 002)
- **Millbeck** — the valley over the shoulder; its men cut peat ("there's no cutting on our side"). (sc 003)
- **The white road** — a dead-straight road running southwest through country that has forgotten it; survives as a dyke too straight for a farmer's sense and a line of hawthorn keeping courtesy to something gone; crosses a bog on a spine of stone that doesn't sink. His feet knew it, eight centuries ago. (sc 003)
- **Wray** — ford and market town on the river, two days southwest by the tops; the only crossing shy of the cloister bridge; lights on the tops past it two nights running, crowds watching. (sc 003)
- **Sorrel's steading** — a green fold at the hawthorn line's far end: hearth, hens, a dog. (sc 003)
- **The bield** — dry-stone shelter where dyke meets hill fold, two miles along the old line; one gable gone, half a turf roof; night 2's camp. (sc 003)
- **The vale of the river** — the first observant country the party has crossed: murmur-politeness, gloved carters, wool dyed in fades with weighted hems, a padlock-shrine at the road-fork; three days into a shrine-mood, the whole vale walking southwest to look at the lights. (sc 004)
- **Wray** (expanded) — ford-town grown on the crossing: the ford wide and shin-deep over pale gravel between ancient stone piers that carry nothing anymore; grey houses stitched with polished iron cramps; on the near bank a way-house of the Order (bronze bell over the door, seal-knot in the lintel, a desk inside); the market cross at the square's upper corner, cracked through its height and stitched with iron polished bright by two centuries of hands; the drover-yard with its taproom and the loft over the pens, one ladder, a hatch to the stall below. (sc 004)
- **The near ridge tide-line** — camps, torch stubs, trampled grass, all stopping along one clean invisible line like froth on a beach; a whole country came up to look, and not one soul went past. (sc 005)
- **The empty country** — long moor-backs running toward the blue hills past the tide-line: larks singing over it, fallen glass glittering patient in the turf, thick as after rain, and no soul in it. (sc 005)
- **The coomb** — a stone bowl a half-mile across in a fold of the blue hills, holding a new flood that has risen every day: a drowned fold-wall running toward the middle (its top a foot under, a walkway for half the distance), fence-posts going under, a bothy roof breaking the surface; the bottom glittering with four nights' falls; no bird crosses it. Near-centered, an island hardly bigger than a threshing floor wearing a ring of fresh-driven seal-iron teeth, bare of any mark; inside the ring, a man-length slab packed with lead wool over an echoing chamber — a drip, an iron ladder, water rising. (sc 005)
- **The chamber** — a stone shaft round as a well, twice his height; a shelf cut square above the old waterline (tallow stub in its own grease, empty hill-made water-skin, the coin); flooded whole by night's end; the coin at the bottom of it now. (sc 006)
- **The long house** — winter quarters of the builder's crew, under the far slope beyond a pass southwest through the blue hills; one long room, turf roof, a hearth half its length; NO iron in the house — wooden latches, bronze cauldron, bone hooks, not one nail; hundreds of marks cut in a roof-beam, one for each pair of hands the work has ever kept. Marked by his hand in the dirt: ~3 days' walk, 4 with a child; the pass shuts in winter. (sc 006)
- **The country per Whin's map** — east/behind: Wray, the ford, the vale — shut (a bell-house at the crossing, the whole vale awake); down the river: towns bigger and thicker, grey brothers at every crossing — "towns eat secrets and sell the bones"; **Tsarnet** — downriver, hearsay: quay-lamps that burn all night with no wick; north: her known, burned country; southwest over the blue hills: unknown-empty — no bells, no roads, no bread; drovers say the river rises in there, low country far on the other side. "Safe-from-the-Order and safe-from-hunger point different ways." (sc 006)
- **The far-side town** — drawn by his unsteered hand: over the pass the road descends the far side in switchbacks ("the hand knows where the turns are; it braked loads down them"); at the bottom a fork — one branch climbs to the long house's valley, the other runs down along a water and stops at a hatch of squares, the builder's sign for houses; the place's name is a burned step. Bread came up that road once — the hand is sure of it; whether anyone bakes there now, eight hundred years on, the hand knows nothing at all. Past the house the road ends: "The road ended at the work. It was the road's reason." (sc 007)

## Things
- **The book** — bound in not-quite-leather the color of late honey, untitled; warm like a live coal; iron dust that lands on it sinks in and is gone, taking something with it; carried wrapped on his back; unread, unremembered. (sc 001)
- **His kit** — a Warden boy's oiled dust-cloak, a robe-strip, a yoke-strap sling, a water-skin, half a black loaf. The iron work-knife stayed with the boy. (sc 001)
- **The drop** — a plum-sized glass remnant holding a grain of his light; leans toward him and steadies near him; Whin's by terms, wrapped in wool inside her coat. (sc 002)
- **His kit, revised** — the cloak went to the Brother; he wears Whin's coarse brown shawl and a mask-cloth torn from the cloak's hem; the loaf is long gone, the water-skin refilled at the tower cistern. (sc 002)
- **The book, opened** — honey leather worn dark to the shape of his own hand; warm as bread; opens flat, used not kept; the old speech, and he can read it. First page, alone, cut deep: *"What you paid, I kept."* Then entries; the one read is a working life patiently mending something enormous in weather — a scaffold on a west face holding through rain, a third pier to watch come spring, men sung steady on high courses. The hand is his; the life does not come in — "like being read a letter about a stranger you are told you loved." *What that life was, and whose, is not settled.* (sc 003)
- **His kit, again revised** — Harrick's coat and shirt (close at the shoulders, short in the sleeve) over the mask-cloth; dressed, he passes for a hill man. (sc 003)
- **Whin's iron bell as screen** — wool in the bell, drop in the wool, bell dead center of the pack: a shout down to a mutter. "Iron drinks what points." The Brother, of the bell: "Keep it away from my skin. That thing is a small hungry nothing." (sc 005)
- **The drop's second lean** — under its old stillness near him, a fine steady tremble pointing at the child — the first time it has pointed at anything but him. Two readings, unresolved on-screen: any glass of his would shiver in country this soaked with another séqa's spilled light, or the child's light and his are close enough kin that the drop cannot tell them apart. (sc 005)
- **The barred-sun coin** — fixed by inspection: heavy for its size, iron heart under silver-grey cladding that sips through the cladding; a tower one face, a sunburst with a bar struck corner to corner the other; the cutting sharp, proud, new — not worn by any pocket. Stirred no memory, honestly waited for. Thrown back into the chamber water at his choice — evidence canon, object gone. (sc 006)
- **The slab, believed light** — the two-voiced Working PERSISTS in the stone; it came up "like a door of dry pine," floated high and flat, carried her lying flat (not him), and served as her raft across three hundred paces of black water. (sc 006)
- **The book, entered** — it has a *down*: palm flat on the page, the room going quiet above "the way the surface of the lake went quiet," and you live the day the ink holds — the better part of an hour of the waking world. The entry read afterwards stands grey, faded like ink twenty years older than its neighbors: **the book is a candle; reading it burns it.** Inside, the day has a grain like wood — deviations land in grooves the man wore himself; dust-eaten names are burned places inside the memory too (a brightness with no face; a name-shaped silence in your own mouth); what the man knew but never wrote is not in the day. (sc 006)
- **The purse, converted to bread-days** — Whin's haul off the pilgrim crowd: meal, a small sack of oats, two flat loaves, a grey blanket that smells of a byre, tallow, twine, a fist of dried fish, rope. "Bread-days. Twelve if we're careful, and I'm always careful." No pack beasts to be had — folk a day out on their own feet own packs, not animals. (sc 007)
- **The slab, unsaid and sunk** — he unsaid the two-voiced lightness alone, palm flat on the wet stone: "*Qavád*," one gold thread sinking in "like rain into ground"; a sound like a book closing, felt in the palm more than heard; the water took it back in "a settling," one gulp, one ring, gone. No toll he could find. Two tins on the shore knocked once against their buckles and went still; nobody turned. (sc 007)

## History
- At dawn in the year 812 of the Long Quiet, Berzhár split and a man walked out. The age that begins with this has no name yet. (sc 001)
- Night 1 of the waking: the qadín came into his first free sleep; his dream-leak rose as sparks through the roofless tower and fell over the west scree as remnants. The west country saw stars fall. (sc 002)
- Day 2, mid-morning to nightfall: the west country gleans the fall into tins; a made Warden faced down Carse in Harrick's yard; the column walked the cloister road west with Carse's tale coming to meet it; past Wray the sky burned rose two nights running with the vale watching. (sc 003)
- Day 3 of the waking: the party crossed Wray's ford at evening inside the pilgrim crowd, unfound; the lights' vale in shrine-mood — crowds walking to the hills with torches two a penny, a preacher loud in murmur country, "the Unsealing" whispered and hushed. (sc 004)
- Night 4 of the lights: the flare made shape — a hillside of whin in full bloom, gold and thorn, stood in the sky over the blue hills for every watching soul between Wray and the mountains; a whole town knelt to a thornbush and made one sound together. (sc 005)
- Dawn, day 4: the lattice flashed on-screen — a tooth of light on the knoll, answered from the way-house roof, answered again from a third hill east — light talking to light about something that crossed the ford. (sc 005)
- Day 4: the gleaner boy pointed the grey brother at the drover-yard with his whole arm. (sc 005)
- Night 4 of the waking / the fifth night of the lights: the sky over the blue hills went dark for the first time in five nights, with a country watching it not happen — and some out there decided that means it's finished. The child was freed, crossed the water on the believing slab, and was named. Torch-folk came over the ridge through the night — three, five, six, seven — and camped on the moor, waiting for morning. (sc 006)
- The Brother's first free deciding: it ate the entire lakebed fall — the child's four nights — before the morning crowd could reach it; the near-shore drops turned toward the sleeping child, all together, before going out. (sc 006)
- The flashback fixed (from inside the book's entered day): he was a builder, a master of works; forty men sent down before the passes shut; the crew wintered at the long house "as every year"; the toast — "To the work — may it never be finished!" — a ritual, fourteen cups, and under the grin half a breath of cold, every year, at the word *finished*. The work: vast, closing its valley's head from side to side, a low hollow wind-sound off it, up behind the house, never seen. (sc 006)
- At first grey of day 5, far east, the lattice flashed its first word of the morning. (sc 006)
- Day 5, morning: the crowd reached the coomb's shore — a score of folk by late morning, small fires, tins on belts, "may your seals hold" traded between strangers; the country's reading of the dark sky said aloud at a fire-ring: "It's finished. The sky said so. Now comes whatever the sky meant by it." (sc 007)
- The grey-horse tale reached the coomb's fire-rings ahead of them — "a grey horse at Wray that ate stars out of a man's tin. Third-hand and wrong in the details, but it walked here faster than we did." (sc 007)
- The gleaner boys saw the slab float and bob; the elder, "nine and worldly," delivered the verdict that guards the party better than any Working: "Stones don't float." (sc 007)
- Day 5, midmorning: camp struck and un-said (ash drowned, blaze muddied, rope halter), the slab sunk honest, and the four walked out southwest on the old road — the Brother banked dim, Qamí's first ride, the crowd letting them go "the way crowds let go of what isn't their story." (sc 007)

## Laws
- Iron sips radiance on contact: seal-bricks numbed and grey-printed his hands; the dust drank his falling blood; the dust slowly eats the book. (sc 001)
- His leakage shows when the light around him fails: breath and words faintly gold; skin glowing at the seams. (sc 001)
- The tilt: a constant bodily pull behind the breastbone toward the newborn wounded one — direction, not distance. Currently southwest, beyond the horizon. (sc 001)
- Craft can survive qorbán with its provenance burned away (his field-surgery); reaching for the burned part has a physical vertigo. (sc 001)
- Vivid dream-leakage falls as remnants: glass-like shapes holding live light. Iron dust eats dream-stuff as it eats the book. (sc 002)
- Escaped light is gone — it cannot be recalled. Attention can thin the leak (a cupped hand around a flame; aches high in the chest, lapses with distraction). Sleep banks the wound. (sc 002)
- The wound can be opened directly, without formula or sign, to pour raw light; the sea on the far side is willing, and opening feels like rest — the toll lands regardless. (sc 002)
- Remnant-light leans toward its source and steadies near him. A completed remnant spends what was poured — a lamp, not a well. (sc 002)
- An oath sworn "by the brightness that lies beyond" has physical purchase: the Brother's seam pulsed like a knot pulled tight; his gate leaned; the oath seated in his chest like a wire. (sc 002)
- Toll scale (Hannu's ruling, sc 002 distill): a séqal's upper limits are very high — breaking an iron mountain took most of a self; warming a hearth is trivial; pouring a Brother full is just noticeable. Bookkeeping follows this scale. (sc 002)
- His body carries a fine old scar through the right brow, older than memory; the Brother copied it true. He has never seen his own face. (sc 002)
- **The needle rule (hardened)** — every remnant of the night's fall leans toward its source, holding bearing like a compass; the lean spikes when he Works; it answers the Brother as it answers him — one light. Distance weakens it; nothing known stops it. (sc 003)
- **Reshaping** — a pour with light-speak can re-form a made remnant into an imagined shape (the Warden boy's face fulfilled: older, taller, a Warden of rank). Holding a shape costs the Brother nothing it can feel; the changing is the cost, paid by the maker. The shaped robe is the light's skin — it passes anywhere short of touch. (sc 003)
- **The link** — touch calms the Brother fully and is a rope; at ten paces it is a thread that carries wanting, not words; the Brother renders the wanting into words of its own. (sc 003)
- **Remnants are the Brother's food** — fallen remnants sink into its skin and refill it somewhat (a candle banked to a hearth); dream-content carried in a remnant passes into its keeping. (sc 003)
- **Sleep and the wound** — awake, attention can cup the wound all day (an ache high in the chest; no toll); sleep is the cupped hand opening — that is what rest is. No craft he retains binds a séqa shut for sleeping; the knowing is a hole. (sc 003)
- **The rain arithmetic (hardened, night 2)** — the Brother can drink an ordinary night dry: nothing reaches the sky, no new fall. A flare-surge outruns it — it has a capacity — and what slips its hands rises and falls as fresh glass where he sleeps. (sc 004)
- **Reshaping extends man→beast** — a pour + light-speak re-formed the Brother into a horse; it weighs what it decides to weigh (a man's-height drop landed soundless); it kept its words when asked to; holding remains free, the changing remains the maker's toll. (sc 004)
- **The link at touch carries near-words** — meaning arriving whole, "the way a thing you already knew surfaces," wearing the Brother's plainness; mind-speech runs both ways (*Like this?*). At touch a rope, as before; the words are new. (sc 004)
- **The Brother cannot pull light** — remnants cross only the last inch to it, themselves, from open containers, if nothing holds them: "I have no wound; I'm not a well they rise toward. I'm only downhill." Anything shut keeps them — tin, wool, a fist. (sc 004)
- **Iron screening** — enough dead metal between source and remnant drinks the lean thin: his floorless memory of hands packing star-glass into iron-lined cases, and the dealer's iron-lined box on-screen — lidded drops silent, dead still. (sc 004)
- **The one-hole fact** — the craft that quieted a source's calling and the craft that bound a séqa shut for sleeping are the same missing craft. It is not two holes in him. It is one. (sc 004)
- **Qel-qelá** — the children's light-game: a bare hint of aperture (two hairs' width) plus continuous whispered coaxing bends light so others see through you. Limits on-screen: it fools eyes, not the nose; the whispering sustains it and gives you away; without the far stronger bindings it holds only moments, and the light quits like a child — all at once, mid-turn. (sc 005)
- **The lamp-craft** — light can be burned as strength: a lamp-body, bone fed like tallow, past all human endurance. It is not rest: a loan against rest, and the collection comes after, with interest; it spends from the purse he cannot see into; the Working rings every needle in reach. (sc 005)
- **Teaching light-speak to another séqa works, and the taught words persist** — *water*, given through the grip, came back "smaller, and yours, and then its own"; a drop of her fall eaten later by the Brother was still saying *water* to itself inside its own falling. (sc 005)
- **The joint two-voiced Working** — two séqal speaking the same words (*tanút* — stone; *qam* — up) share one Working: "the stone hears two voices, one cracked and one new, saying the same true thing." Shares are not equal by default: his was the teaching, hers the lifting — per Hannu's calibration ruling (at feedback), the lid was completely effortless for the newborn even iron-drained, once she knew how, while he was too exhausted and too iron-drunk for another stone Working of his own. (sc 005)
- **The Brother can eat another séqa's fall, and keeps its content** — the coomb road's glass: "Not yours. Cold water and small dreams. It tastes like what I keep." (sc 005)
- **The Brother can bank its own seam-light when taught** — a shape sent down the link (the cupped hands, four counts, the shielding attention) and the seam dims like a lamp being shaded by someone learning where the shade is. (sc 005)
- **Emanant-senses stop at an iron ring** — "The middle of that island is a hole in me… The ring drinks. I cannot hear past it." Everything in the bowl leans there and goes silent at the iron. (sc 005)
- **A séqa child's flare can be spent into a Working instead of the sky** — given words and a channel, the peak pours into the telling: the whin-bloom (the fourth flare made shape), the slab (a river finding the channel already cut). (sc 005)
- **The touch between two séqal runs both ways** — skin to skin, "something that runs both ways between two of a kind": it carries showing the way the rope does (the shape of a craft, sent and taken), and lent steadiness costs nothing. (sc 006)
- **A joint Working's lightness persists** — "the stone still believes what two voices told it. It has not stopped." Stone changes by Working are not fickle but standing. (sc 006)
- **The ring's teeth sip at a standing Working carried between them** — the slab gained a few pounds passing the teeth, then settled once clear: the ring drinks Workings the way it drinks light. (sc 006)
- **The lamp-craft's collection, landed on-screen** — it announces as warmth leaving the arms from inside; work draws it down faster; it lands whole with the interest, harder than it was borrowed, and drops you where you stand — "between one stroke and the next," in deep water; sleep afterwards comes "by force." (sc 006; closes the sc 005 owed item)
- **A séqa child's solo Working cannot lift a man** — her *QAM* took true hold of the whole of him and slowed his sinking at the cost of everything she had: embers do nothing solo at that scale. (sc 006)
- **The release of a kept word** — the giver can unsay a keeping; it then belongs to the Brother to decide, and it weighs the deciding ("a pause exactly the length of a thing being weighed"). **A keeping decided once is a memory, not a rule.** The third word's space stands open at his choice; the Brother decides each time. "Kept. Not the way I keep rules. Some other way. I will find out which." (sc 006)
- **The Brother walks on water** — it weighs what it decides, standing on the surface; it carried a grown man out across its back, teeth in his forearm, "hard enough to bruise, horse-plain." (sc 006)
- **The book's laws** — the down; the candle-price (a read entry spent grey); the grain; the burned names inside; the unwritten absent. (Whether a spent entry can be relived: NOT ruled — held soft.) (sc 006)
- **The tilt falls silent on arrival** — the compass has arrived; for the first time since the mountain, no pull behind the breastbone, "strange as a missing tooth." (sc 006)
- **Séqal sustenance (Hannu's ruling, sc 006 distill)** — in the very early stages of a wound's opening, the wound sustains the body; as the puncture settles and grows less raw, the physical needs return. (Relates to the séqal's subconscious protection from harm — that protection itself stays soft; see questions.md.) (ruling 2026-07-13)
- **A séqa's opening burns holes in retained speech** — the wound's qorbán reaches into the common tongue: most speech survives, comes slowly, and has burned places at the wound-adjacent words; the bearer knows a burned place from a forgotten one from inside. (sc 007; observed in Qamí, consistent with his own holes)
