## The protagonist's arc
- The wound is both literal (the séqa) and characterological. **Misbelief:** *"the wound of my mind cannot be healed — and must not be, because my power is needed to protect the world."* **True want:** healing — of himself, and of the generational trauma of the openings.
- The opening image is the thesis, deliberately unsubtle: he carries the weight of a mountain and must push it aside.
- **The discovery he is moving toward:** the world can be healed without carrying the wound, without absolute power — through connection with humans; and in this new era there are humans who can do this.
- The death-shaped trap (worldbible §7): "healed" feels like *finished, at rest, complete* — which is iron, which is death. The learning is to stay unfinished.

## Reconciling "some of his mind will return" with scripture
Scripture is firm: pieces burned as qorbán do not come back (§5.5). So what "returns" is never regeneration. Two honest channels:
1. **Rest scars the wound half-shut** — leakage calms, control returns, the furnace dims toward a person.
2. **Identity is re-told from outside** — the book he spoke into being, places, witnesses, records, enemies, the companion. He can only be *re-membered* through others. This IS the theme; never cheat it with spontaneous total recall. Flashbacks are played as scenes, triggered by external things.

Hannu's calibration (margin, sc 002 distill, verbatim): "shattering *an iron mountain* (still need to think a bit about the mechanics of this) took most of the Sealed One's memories, and some of these will slowly return as the wound dims with sleep and rest, although there will be scars. We have established that the upper limits of a séqal's powers are quite high, so for bookkeeping purposes these are quite minor workings so far; perhaps the pouring just about noticeable (if not in Sealed One's current state), the hearth-warming trivial."

## Figures (working sketches — sheets are born in play)
- **The Opener** — his wound-parent: a large figure of an earlier generation (not necessarily the First Opener). Made him; the relationship has the texture of both parenthood and abuse; also an emblem of what it costs to try to save the world. Flashback scenes should establish and reinforce the misbelief.
- **The companion** — younger female; street urchin or misfit in the rigid, hierarchical society of the Long Quiet; does *mechanism*, not Working (the seed's inversion: the child raises the god). Helps him recover his past.
- **The other Sealed** — few in number, important, largely antagonistic; some are waking. He may need alliances with some of them.
- **The lonely one** — a waking Sealed whose wound is loneliness: wants others of their kind awake, and may be the cause of the wakings.

## The situation (why now)
- Someone has rediscovered or performed an opening: **a newborn wounded one exists.** Who, where, and by whose hand — to be discovered in play. A cataclysmic fact; wars will be fought to destroy or to subvert this person.
- The protagonist's single retained imperative on waking: *find the newborn wounded one.*
- **Myth vs truth of the Sealing:** the age believes the people rose together and caged the wounded in a mighty battle. Likelier truth: the sleeper *helped the world* — the myth is a necessary lie. He may have left a mechanism to wake him if another Sealed stirred — or, more likely, if a new wounded one came into being. (Hidden-soft until contact hardens it.)
