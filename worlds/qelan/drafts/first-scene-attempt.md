Under the iron mountain, he sleeps. 

His lips are sealed with wax. But the light in him still spills out, even in sleep, even through the iron mask, the manacles and the metal mittens that binds his hands. 

the wound is deep, the wound is bright, and there is always leakage. 

it makes air for him to breathe. it sustains the cells of his body. it makes small echoes of his dreams, in this coffin-like space where he lies, under Berzhár the iron-mount. his skin glows, ever so faintly, every so often, tiny flecks of radiance drift up like sparks from a golden fire. If he is dreaming a vivid dream, they may become things, even without the shaping-speak, and so the tiny tomb is littered with their remnants. a dry red leaf. a needle. a child’s clay doll. 

some of the radiance disappears into the blackness of the walls. they have carved pathways into it over the centuries, in the shape of his dreams, so if you were to cut through Berzhár with a sword, the cross-section would be a tree, a garden of forking paths, of memory and dream. 

still, the tons of dead star-matter around him are uncountable. Hatumin still seek out and collect it in all the world’s reaches, purify it in the monastery’s forges, smelt it into dark, heavy bricks and climb the stairs set in the mountain’s sides to add them to the pile. 

Eight hundred and twelve years he has slept, the years of the Long Quiet, all the years after the last war and the Sealwright. His iron-muffled dreams are unquiet. This particular one is a recurring one, a nightmare. The chains clink as he turns, and he moans through wax-and-iron-sealed lips. 

A spark of radiance escapes and drifts upwards, through the network of passages his dreams have carved in the iron. A black brick absorbs it, eroded ever so slightly. but a passage has been made. 

outside the sun, Semmá, rises. the spark rises towards it, accelerates, shoots to the sky like a reverse falling star.
