- **The setting of a séqa** *(believed)* — a séqa sets like a habit, not a scar: the wound is a door forced through the boundary between self and Fullness, and what "settles" is what the mind learns in its first days about the door — what opens it, whether it closes, whose it is. A fresh séqa stays keyed to the state that made it unless retaught; "knits wrong" is the wrong lessons hardening. Three things must set right: *boundary* (this passes through me; it is not me) · *agency* (I decide whether and how far it opens) · *closure* (opening is reversible; I can come back) — and the way home is taught before the door is used: a new séqa's first taught act is a Not-Working. (study 2026-07-16, Hannu's seed, ratified; converges with the sustenance ruling — one settling clock, three faces: needs return, knitting proceeds, keys harden)
- **Qavrút — the First Closure** *(believed)* — a torn-open mind cannot govern its own attention; another séqa lends a stable pattern *beside* the wound, never repair inside it, for only another séqa can feel the aperture move. The maxim: *a fresh wound must be held by two minds until it learns the boundaries of one.* The helper lends a boundary and must not become the boundary — a closure the helper dominates sets the wound to his voice and hands. The rite never asks what was seen through the door — coordinates before testimony: "you do not have to say what you saw; first show me you can come back." Iron buys quiet while control is learned; it never sets. Sahíric: **qavrút** (Q–V–R + the settling *-ut*, "what companionship settles") — done once, in an emergency, by whoever is there; **qavrá** is what it becomes for life. (study 2026-07-16, ratified)
- **šuvá** *(believed; unspoken at the table until taught)* — "come back, return" (Š–V–ʾ): the sister-word of *patá*; the Not-Working in a child's mouth; the taught cycle *patá … šuvá* — open, and return. Distinct from *rafá*, the mend — closure is taught before mending is ever attempted. (study 2026-07-16, ratified)
- **The failure keys** *(believed)* — species of the one setting-law: *fright-keyed* (opens reflexively under fear; power indistinguishable from panic) · *wrath-keyed* (rage the only fuel; grievances cultivated) · *dissociation-keyed* (opens by loosening the self; unusually costly in qorbán) · *leaky* (never fully closes; the Fullness intrudes on ordinary thought) · *overclosed* (suppressed so hard it opens only under catastrophe; looks cured until it detonates) · *mirror-set* (closes only in the teacher's presence). The Order's iron rooms read as an overclosing tradition; the Codex's own word for its quieting may be **séqút** (S–Q–ʾ + *-ut*, "the wound, settled") — done *to* you with iron, where qavrút is done *with* you by a second mind. (study 2026-07-16, ratified; which key, if any, is anyone's stays unwritten)
