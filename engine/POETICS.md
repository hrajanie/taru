# POETICS.md — The Seed Credo

What the first world's play proved about what makes a story at this table good. A world's back room keeps its own credo (`gm/poetics.md`) — argued from its own scenes, cited or it doesn't count — but it starts from here rather than from nothing. Argue with these too; outgrow them where the story earns it.

## Scenes
- Bargains are scenes. A negotiation summarized is a scene wasted; play it.
- Wonder lands through domestic gesture, not spectacle.
- Clarity is the precondition of dread. Nothing frightens from inside a fog of prose.
- A counted beat yields a reading, not a number.
- End on an image, never on a resolution (GM.md).

## Choices
- A choice is only as hard as its best exit is expensive. Price the third path the player will invent; don't wall the obvious ones.
- Stakes must be legible before the choice is made — and the dearest stakes hang on second persons, not the protagonist alone.
- Avoided peril converts to debt, never to nothing. Teeth routed around bite later.
- A crucible is exits closing, each closure paid on-screen — never a wall narrated into place.

## People
- NPCs know things and want things; they are never exposition with a face (GM.md).
- A want must cast a shadow: a desire shown must cost or complicate something.
- The body is the truth-detector: read a person's state through physical handling and somatic signal, never narrated interiority.
- Character is what craft reveals under practice — dimension arrives through what someone does with what they're taught, at visible prices.

## The hidden world
- Holes are load-bearing. An absence deliberately kept can carry more than a fact stated.
- In a mystery: evidence before person, two jaws, never a swap — let evidence and presence close together; don't trade one revelation for another.
- A coincidence must be the world's before it is the story's: an echo reads as authorial neatness unless it was planted in the world first.
- A standing charge is lived pressure, not a filed fact: if the story owes something, the character must feel the owing.
- The world's machinery may rescue, at a visible price; the narration never does (GM.md).
- Every prep leaves the next scene wanting to exist.
